# STAKEHOLDERS.md — Campus Lost & Found System (CLAFS)

## Stakeholder Analysis

This document identifies the key stakeholders of the Campus Lost & Found System (CLAFS), their roles, concerns, pain points, and measurable success metrics.

---

## Stakeholder Analysis Table

| # | Stakeholder | Role | Key Concerns | Pain Points | Success Metrics |
|---|-------------|------|--------------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Student (Item Loser)** | Primary user who has lost a personal item on campus and needs to report it and track recovery | Wants a fast, easy way to report a lost item and be notified when it is found. Concerned about privacy of personal details shared in the report. | Currently has to visit multiple campus offices or check physical notice boards with no guarantee the item has been logged. No way to track status of their report. | 80% of students able to submit a lost item report in under 3 minutes. At least 60% of reported lost items matched and returned within 7 days. |
| 2 | **Student (Item Finder)** | Primary user who has found someone else's item on campus and wants to return it responsibly | Wants a simple way to report a found item without taking personal responsibility for holding it. Needs confidence that the rightful owner will be verified before handover. | No centralized platform to report found items. Often unsure who to hand items to, leading to items being left unattended or handed to the wrong person. | 90% of found item reports submitted within 24 hours of discovery. Zero cases of items handed to unverified claimants. |
| 3 | **Campus Security / Admin** | System administrator who manages all item reports, reviews claims, approves or rejects them, and marks cases as resolved | Needs a centralized dashboard to manage all reports efficiently. Wants to reduce physical queues at the security office and maintain a digital audit trail of all cases. | Currently relies on a manual register (paper or basic spreadsheet) to track lost and found items. High volume of in-person visits consumes staff time. No automated notifications to relevant parties. | 50% reduction in in-person lost and found queries at the security office within one semester of launch. 100% of cases having a digital audit trail. |
| 4 | **University IT Department** | Technical stakeholder responsible for deploying, maintaining, and securing the system infrastructure | Concerned about system security, data privacy compliance, uptime, and ease of maintenance. Wants a system that is easy to deploy and update without heavy infrastructure investment. | New systems often arrive without documentation, making maintenance difficult. Security vulnerabilities in student-facing apps are a recurring concern. Limited IT staff to manage complex deployments. | System achieves 99% uptime during business hours. Zero critical security incidents in the first academic year. Deployment completable by one IT staff member in under 2 hours. |
| 5 | **Lecturer / Academic Staff** | Secondary user who may lose or find items on campus and uses the system the same way students do, but also has an elevated trust level in the verification process | Wants a straightforward experience similar to students but expects their staff status to add credibility when verifying ownership of items. Concerned about data accuracy. | Same manual process frustrations as students. Staff items (e.g., laptops, access cards) are higher value and need more careful handling. | Staff-reported items resolved within 5 business days. Staff trust level correctly reflected in the claim verification process 100% of the time. |
| 6 | **University Management / Administration** | Strategic stakeholder who oversees campus operations and student experience. Does not interact with the system daily but cares about outcomes | Wants improved student satisfaction and a reduction in complaints about lost property. Interested in usage statistics and system impact reports. Concerned about reputational risk if student data is mishandled. | No visibility into how many items are lost and found on campus. No data to inform decisions about high-risk areas (e.g., which buildings or times have the most lost items). | 20% improvement in student satisfaction scores related to campus services within one academic year. Monthly admin reports generated automatically with zero manual effort. |
| 7 | **New Students (First-Year / International)** | Vulnerable user group unfamiliar with campus layout and existing processes | Needs an intuitive, easy-to-navigate interface that does not require prior knowledge of campus processes. May need multilingual support or simplified language. | Existing manual processes are intimidating and unclear for new students who do not know where to go or who to speak to. | 95% of new students able to complete a report without requiring external help. System usability score (SUS) of at least 80/100. |

---

## Summary of Stakeholder Priorities

| Priority | Concern | Stakeholders Affected |
|----------|---------|----------------------|
| High | Fast and easy item reporting | Students, Lecturers |
| High | Secure claim verification | Campus Security, IT, Management |
| High | System uptime and reliability | IT Department, Campus Security |
| Medium | Audit trail and reporting | Campus Security, Management |
| Medium | Data privacy and security | IT Department, Management, All Users |
| Low | Multilingual / accessibility support | New Students |
